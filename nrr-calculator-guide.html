What is Net Run Rate (NRR)?

Net Run Rate is the universal tie-breaker in modern cricket tournaments. If two teams finish the group stages with the same amount of points (e.g., both won 4 matches), the team with the higher NRR advances.

The Exact NRR Formula

The math seems simple but gets complicated with fractions of an over:

NRR = (Total Runs Scored / Total Overs Faced) - (Total Runs Conceded / Total Overs Bowled)

The trap most people fall into is calculating 3.3 overs as "3.3" in a calculator, when mathematically it's actually 3.5 overs (3 overs and 3/6ths of an over). If a team is bowled out, the full quota of scheduled overs is counted against them.
