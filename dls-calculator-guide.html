What is the DLS (Duckworth-Lewis-Stern) Method?

The DLS method is a mathematical formula used in limited-overs cricket to calculate the target score for the team batting second in a match interrupted by weather or other circumstances. It ensures fairness by analyzing two resources: remaining overs and remaining wickets.

How the Calculator Works

Instead of manually doing complex math, the Gully Scorer DLS calculator does it for you. If it rains during the first innings, the app adjusts the target based on overs lost. If it rains during the chase, it gives you the exact "Par Score" needed to win.

  • Step 1: Enter the total overs scheduled for the match.
  • Step 2: Input the interruptions (overs bowled and wickets lost when play stopped).
  • Step 3: The app instantly provides the revised target for the chasing team.

Bringing Fair Play to Local Matches

Before digital apps, local tournaments either had to abandon matches or use unfair run-rate comparisons. With our integrated calculator, your local league can handle rain delays just like professional international matches.
